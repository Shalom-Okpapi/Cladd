Technical Requirements

File Specifications

Getting your file right before upload saves time and ensures your design prints exactly as you intend it to.

Accepted File Formats

  • PNG — Preferred. Supports transparency, ideal for designs with no background.
  • JPG / JPEG — Accepted. Best for photographic or full-colour designs. No transparency support.
  • SVG — Accepted for vector-based designs. Scales without quality loss.

Resolution & Size

Product Recommended Resolution Min DPI
Power Bank Pouch 3000 × 3000 px 300 DPI
Phone Pouch 2400 × 3200 px 300 DPI
Sticker Sheet 3000 × 3000 px 300 DPI

Low resolution designs will be rejected. Always design at 300 DPI or above. What looks sharp on screen can appear blurry in print at lower resolutions.

Colour Mode

Design in RGB colour mode. Our printing process converts to CMYK, and our system handles that conversion. Designing in CMYK can produce unexpected colour shifts.

Safe Zone & Bleed

Keep all important elements — text, faces, logos — at least 5mm away from the edges of your canvas. The outer edge may be trimmed slightly during production. Anything in the bleed area is not guaranteed to appear on the final product.

Colour

Colour Guidelines

Colour is the first thing a customer notices. Getting it right makes the difference between a design that scrolls past and one that stops someone mid-browse.

Contrast Sells

Designs with high contrast between the main subject and background tend to perform better. A bold subject on a clean background is almost always more eye-catching than a busy, low-contrast composition.

Use Transparent Backgrounds Where Possible

A PNG with a transparent background gives customers a feel for how the design sits on the actual product colour. It also looks more professional in your shop preview.

Avoid Pure White Backgrounds on White Products

If a product variant is white, a pure white background will be invisible. Use a light grey (`#f5f5f5`) as a placeholder when previewing, but export with transparency.

Colour Consistency Across Devices

Screens display colour differently. What looks vibrant on your monitor may appear slightly duller in print. Use a colour profile like sRGB IEC61966-2.1 for the most consistent results across devices and print.

Layout

Composition Tips

A well-composed design feels intentional. Here's what works on physical products specifically.

Centre-Weighted Designs Work Best on Pouches

The main subject of your design should sit near the centre of the canvas. Customers see the middle of the product first. Edge details often get less attention — and may be trimmed.

Leave Room for the Product to Breathe

Designs that fill every centimetre of the canvas can feel claustrophobic on a physical product. Whitespace or negative space isn't wasted space — it makes your subject stand out more.

Text on Products

If your design includes text, keep it short, bold, and legible at small sizes. Avoid thin script fonts smaller than 18pt — they become unreadable in print. Test your design at 10% of its original size on screen to simulate how it looks at product scale.

Think About It From All Angles

A power bank pouch gets rotated, turned, and held in all directions. Consider how your design looks both upright and sideways. Symmetric or direction-neutral designs tend to work better for this reason.

Strategy

What Sells on Cladd

Beyond the technical, here's what we see working in the marketplace.

Identity-Driven Designs

Cladd customers aren't buying a pouch — they're buying something that looks like them. The designs that sell best are the ones people feel represent them. Niche interests, strong cultural references, specific aesthetics. The more specific, the more powerful.

Consistent Series Outperform One-Offs

Artists who build a visual series — five to ten designs with a unified style, colour palette, or theme — tend to generate more sales than those with a scattered portfolio. When a customer loves one design, they look at your other work. Give them a reason to buy more than one.

Seasonal & Timely Themes

Designs tied to seasons, cultural moments, or trending aesthetics spike in popularity when relevant. Plan ahead — upload seasonal designs at least three weeks before the relevant date.

Abstract Over Literal

Designs that suggest a feeling or identity without being too literal tend to have wider appeal. A design that says "I love jazz" in words reaches fewer people than one that feels like jazz without spelling it out.

Your Presence

Building Your Brand on Cladd

Your Shop Name Matters

Choose a shop name that reflects your style and is easy to remember. Avoid numbers or underscores that make it harder to search for. Your shop name is your brand — treat it like one.

Write a Real Bio

Customers connect with artists who feel like real people. A two-sentence bio that explains who you are and what drives your work is more valuable than a blank profile. It creates trust before they even see your designs.

Upload a Profile Photo

Shops with profile photos consistently outperform those without. It doesn't have to be a headshot — it can be your logo, a piece of your art, or any image that represents you.

Promote Your Shop

Cladd gives you the platform, but you bring the audience. Share your shop link on Instagram, Pinterest, TikTok, and wherever your community lives. Every external visitor who discovers your work through you is a customer Cladd didn't have to find.

Before You Upload

Pre-Upload Checklist

Run through this before every upload to avoid rejections and ensure your design looks its best.

  • File format: PNG, JPG, or SVG
  • Resolution: 300 DPI or above
  • Colour mode: RGB
  • Canvas size: At least 3000 × 3000 px for pouches
  • Safe zone: All key elements are 5mm from edges
  • Transparency: Background is transparent (PNG) where applicable
  • Originality: All elements are your own work or properly licensed
  • No copyrighted characters or logos you don't own
  • Text is legible at small sizes
  • File size: Under 25MB

All good? Head to your dashboard and upload your design.
