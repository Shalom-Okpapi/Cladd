Your Work is Yours

Your Ownership Rights

When you upload a design to Cladd, you keep full ownership of your original artwork. Cladd does not claim creative ownership of any design you create and upload.

By listing on Cladd, you grant us a non-exclusive, worldwide licence to display, reproduce, and sell products featuring your design. This licence exists only to allow us to operate the marketplace on your behalf. It does not transfer ownership or prevent you from selling your work elsewhere.

Your art belongs to you. Always has. Always will. Cladd is just the platform that sells it.

You're Good to Go

What You Can Upload

You may upload designs that contain:

  • 100% original artwork — illustrations, paintings, digital art, photography, or any creative work you made yourself
  • Licensed content — artwork, fonts, or elements you have a commercial licence to use in print-on-demand products. Keep a copy of your licence on file.
  • Public domain content — works where copyright has expired. Note that some public domain works have been renewed or have separate trademark protection — verify before using.
  • Abstract patterns and textures — original geometric, abstract, or decorative designs you created
  • Lettering and typography — original hand-lettered or typographic work using fonts you own or have licensed
Hard Stops

What You Cannot Upload

The following are prohibited on Cladd and will result in immediate removal and possible account suspension:

Copyrighted Characters & Franchises

You may not upload designs featuring characters, logos, or imagery from copyrighted franchises — Disney, Marvel, DC, Nintendo, anime franchises, sports leagues, music artists, or any other intellectual property you do not own. This includes fan art intended for commercial sale.

Fan art is not exempt. Selling fan art on a commercial marketplace without a licence is copyright infringement, regardless of how it is framed.

Trademarked Logos & Brand Names

You may not use brand logos, names, or slogans that are trademarked without explicit written permission from the trademark holder. This includes luxury fashion brands, sports brands, tech companies, and any other trademarked entity.

Celebrity Likeness

Designs featuring the recognisable likeness of real people — including public figures, celebrities, athletes, and musicians — are not permitted without their consent.

Content Created by AI Without Rights Clearance

AI-generated designs are permitted on Cladd only if you have verified that the output does not contain elements derived from copyrighted training data in a way that would constitute infringement. You are responsible for the content you upload.

Offensive & Harmful Content

Designs containing hate speech, explicit content, content promoting violence, or content targeting individuals or groups based on protected characteristics are strictly prohibited.

Seen Something Wrong?

Reporting Copyright Infringement

If you believe a design on Cladd infringes your copyright or trademark, you can file a report with us. We take all reports seriously and investigate each one.

What to Include in Your Report

  • Your name, email address, and relationship to the intellectual property (owner or authorised representative)
  • A description of the copyrighted work you believe has been infringed
  • The URL of the infringing listing on Cladd
  • A statement that you have a good-faith belief the use is not authorised by the rights holder
  • A statement that the information in your report is accurate

How to Submit

Send your report to us via the Contact Page and select "Copyright Concern" as the topic. We aim to respond within 3 business days.

False reports have consequences. Filing a false or bad-faith infringement report may expose you to legal liability. Only submit a report if you genuinely believe infringement has occurred.

If Your Design Was Removed

Counter-Notice

If your design was removed following an infringement report and you believe the removal was made in error, you may submit a counter-notice.

What to Include

  • Your name and email address
  • Identification of the design that was removed and its location before removal
  • A statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good-faith belief the design was removed as a result of mistake or misidentification
  • Any supporting documentation — original files, creation dates, licences

How to Submit

Contact us with the subject "Counter-Notice" and include all relevant information. We'll review your counter-notice and notify both parties of the outcome within 10 business days.

Cladd is not a legal authority. We review counter-notices in good faith but are not able to make binding legal determinations. If a copyright dispute is serious, we recommend seeking independent legal advice.
